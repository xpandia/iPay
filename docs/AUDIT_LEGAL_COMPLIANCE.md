Colombia and Brazil are implementing FATF standards\n\nFor qualifying transfers, iPay must collect and transmit: originator name, originator account number (wallet address), originator physical address or date of birth, beneficiary name, and beneficiary account number.\n\n**Technical implication:** The current on-chain transaction structure records only wallet addresses. Off-chain Travel Rule data exchange infrastructure (e.g., integration with Notabene, Chainalysis, or Sygna) is required.\n\n### 2.6 Wallet-Based Identity\n\niPay **cannot** rely solely on wallet-based identity. While blockchain analytics can provide risk scores for wallet addresses, no jurisdiction accepts wallet ownership as a substitute for traditional KYC. However, wallet-based identity can supplement KYC through:\n- Proof-of-wallet-ownership as an additional authentication factor\n- On-chain transaction history for behavioral risk scoring\n- Integration with decentralized identity protocols (e.g., Civic, World ID) as a KYC front-end\n\n---\n\n## 3. iPAY TOKEN CLASSIFICATION\n\n### 3.1 Howey Test Analysis\n\nThe Howey test (SEC v. W.J. Howey Co., 328 U.S. 293 (1946)) asks four questions:\n\n| Prong | Analysis for iPAY | Risk Level |\n|-------|-------------------|------------|\n| **Investment of money** | Users do not directly purchase iPAY. Tokens are auto-minted as a byproduct of payment transactions. No separate investment decision. | LOW |\n| **Common enterprise** | iPAY tokens are minted from a single mint authority controlled by the platform. Token value is tied to the platform's success. Horizontal commonality may exist. | MODERATE |\n| **Expectation of profits** | If iPAY is marketed as increasing in value or is tradeable on secondary markets, this prong is met. If restricted to discounts at merchants (like store credit), this prong fails. | DEPENDS ON IMPLEMENTATION |\n| **Efforts of others** | The platform team controls the mint, sets the loyalty rate, and determines redemption terms. This prong is likely met. | MODERATE-HIGH |\n\n**Overall assessment:** iPAY sits on the boundary. As currently designed (auto-minted, redeemable for discounts at merchants), it more closely resembles loyalty points than a security. However, several features push it toward securities classification:\n\n- The `redeemLoyalty` instruction converts tokens to SOL value at the merchant, creating a monetary redemption pathway\n- If iPAY tokens become tradeable on DEXes (Raydance, Jupiter), secondary market liquidity creates an expectation of profit\n- The platform team controls all parameters (mint rate, redemption rate)\n\n### 3.2 Comparison to Analogous Programs\n\n| Program | Structure | Classification | iPay Comparison |\n|---------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|\n| **Blackbird $FLY** | Earned at restaurants, redeemable across network, built on Base L3 | Not registered as security; structured as loyalty points with limited transferability | Similar model; iPAY should follow FLY's approach |\n| **Starbucks Stars** | Non-transferable, no secondary market, redeemable only within Starbucks ecosystem | Loyalty points (not a token) | iPAY is on-chain and potentially transferable, creating higher risk |\n| **Airline Miles** | Non-transferable (officially), redeemable for flights/upgrades | Consumer rewards; not a security | iPAY's on-chain nature makes it inherently more transferable |\n\n### 3.3 Recommendations to Avoid Securities Classification\n\n1. **Never market iPAY as an investment.** All communications must frame it as \"loyalty rewards\" or \"merchant credits,\" never as something that will \"increase in value.\"\n2. **Restrict transferability.** Implement transfer restrictions in the SPL token program -- soulbound or limited to platform-approved transfers only. The current implementation allows unrestricted transfers.\n3. **Remove monetary redemption.** The `redeemLoyalty` instruction that converts iPAY to SOL/discount value should be restructured as merchant-specific discounts (e.g., \"redeem 100 iPAY for 10% off at Merchant X\") rather than a general SOL-equivalent value.\n4. **No secondary market listings.** Do not list iPAY on any DEX or CEX. Do not provide or encourage liquidity pools.\n5. **Decentralize governance over time.** Reduce the platform team's unilateral control over mint rate, supply, and redemption parameters.\n6. **Do not conduct a token sale.** iPAY should only be distributed as earned rewards, never sold.\n\n---\n\n## 4. DATA PRIVACY\n\n### 4.1 On-Chain Data -- Critical Architectural Issue\n\nThe `registerMerchant` instruction stores `name` (string) and `metadata` (string) directly on-chain in the Merchant PDA account. On-chain data is:\n- **Permanent and immutable** -- cannot be deleted or modified in the traditional sense\n- **Publicly visible** -- anyone can read Solana account data\n- **Globally accessible** -- no jurisdiction-based access controls\n\nThis creates direct conflicts with:\n\n**GDPR (EU):** Article 17 grants the \"right to erasure.\" The EDPB's Guidelines 02/2025 (April 2025) explicitly state that \"technical impossibility cannot be invoked to justify non-compliance\" with erasure rights for blockchain-stored personal data. The EDPB strongly recommends **off-chain storage with on-chain hash references only.**\n\n**Colombia Ley 1581 of 2012 (Habeas Data):** Grants individuals the right to know, update, and rectify information gathered about them. On-chain immutable storage violates this right. Circular 001 of 2025 specifically addresses fintech data handling obligations.\n\n**Brazil LGPD (Lei 13,709/2018):** Article 18 grants data subjects the right to deletion. Similar to GDPR, immutable on-chain storage is incompatible.\n\n### 4.2 Recommended Architecture\n\n**Mandatory redesign:**\n- Store all personal/business data (merchant names, addresses, contact information, KYC documents) **off-chain** in a mutable, encrypted database\n- On-chain, store only **cryptographic hashes** or opaque identifiers (e.g., `merchant_id: [u8; 32]` as a hash of the off-chain record)\n- Implement \"encryption key disposal\" for extreme cases -- store encrypted references on-chain; destroy the key to achieve functional deletion\n- Implement geographic data residency controls (e.g., Colombian merchant data stored in Colombia-based infrastructure)\n\n---\n\n## 5. SMART CONTRACT LEGAL ISSUES\n\n### 5.1 Liability for Smart Contract Bugs\n\nThe iPay smart contract is the **sole custodian of payment logic**. If a bug in `processPayment` causes funds to be lost, misdirected, or locked, iPay (the legal entity) bears liability. Key concerns:\n\n- **No upgrade mechanism visible in the IDL.** If the program is non-upgradeable, bugs cannot be fixed post-deployment. This is a material risk.\n- **Fallback to SystemProgram.transfer** (line 160 of `actions.ts`) -- if the program fails, payments silently fall back to direct SOL transfers without fee collection or loyalty minting. This means merchants might not receive expected loyalty program benefits, creating a breach-of-contract claim.\n- The `processPayment` instruction handles SOL directly -- a reentrancy bug or arithmetic overflow could drain user funds.\n\n### 5.2 Dispute Resolution\n\niPay has **no chargeback mechanism** -- once a Solana transaction is confirmed, it is final. This creates:\n- **Consumer protection violations** in jurisdictions requiring refund rights (EU Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU provides a 14-day withdrawal right for distance contracts)\n- **Merchant disputes** with no on-chain arbitration mechanism\n- **Error recovery** -- if a consumer pays the wrong merchant or wrong amount, there is no programmatic recourse\n\n**Recommendation:** Implement an off-chain dispute resolution mechanism with an escrow option in the smart contract for disputed payments. Consider integration with an ODR (Online Dispute Resolution) platform.\n\n### 5.3 Terms of Service Requirements\n\niPay must publish and require acceptance of:\n- **Merchant Agreement:** Defining fee structure, settlement terms, liability limitations, dispute resolution procedures, data handling obligations\n- **Consumer Terms of Use:** Risk disclosures (price volatility, irreversibility of transactions, smart contract risk), refund policy, privacy policy\n- **Loyalty Program Terms:** Clear statement that iPAY tokens are not securities, not investments, have no guaranteed value, and are subject to modification or termination\n\n---\n\n## 6.
